The Thread Begins

In the summer of 2003, I walked into a Tower Records with some friends and left with something that would change my life forever. It wasn’t a book or a CD. It was the simplest thing — a squeak toy.

As a fan of JTHM, I Feel Sick, Squee, and yes, even Invader Zim, finding something from Jhonen Vasquez’s world was worth whatever money I had at sixteen. There on a shelf, it called out to me: “Spooky the Thing What Squeeks.” I felt drawn to it — beyond fandom. Like it had been waiting for me.

Later that day, at my friend’s house, I just stared at it. And something shifted. The thing reminded me of myself — and I couldn’t explain why. I felt the sudden, strange desire to call myself “Spooky.” But it wasn’t complete. Like Jack Skellington… it needed a last name.

Then I saw it, sitting on my friend’s shelf: a game box for American McGee’s Alice. And I knew. “McGee… Spooky McGee.” That was it. That was me.

By that fall, I had joined DeviantArt — a completely different kind of place back then. A real online art community. I needed a name. It was simple: Spooky McGee.
The First Stitch

He started showing up in sketches, scribbles, and dream-journals. I didn’t have a full story yet — just fragments. Scenes of him standing in streets alone. Smiling through stitched lips. Watching things unravel from rooftops.

The tone was strange. Sometimes silly, sometimes sad. But Spooky never left. Even when the stories didn’t make sense… he was always there. A quiet constant in a loud, shapeless time.
Ravens Immortal

In 2023, everything changed. After years of grief, heartbreak, and creative silence, I prayed for clarity — and what came back was a vision. A desert. A mirror. A stitched man in a top hat. A broken boy standing in the sand. That was the spark.

That’s how Ravens Immortal was born. And that’s when Spooky McGee stopped being a background character. He became a myth, a prophet, a mentor — and a father. He became my throughline. The one who guides others through heartbreak because he’s sewn through his own.
